# Movement Patterns Protocol

## Purpose

Provides frameworks for ensuring balanced training stimulus across fundamental human movement patterns. Prevents imbalances, reduces injury risk, and ensures comprehensive physical development by covering all major movement categories.

## When to Use

- Designing program exercise architecture (program-creator)
- Selecting exercises for individual workouts (workout-creator)
- Evaluating if program/workout is balanced (all agents)
- Identifying gaps in exercise selection
- Planning accessory work to address imbalances

## Decision Framework

### The 7 Fundamental Movement Patterns

**1. Squat Pattern** (Bilateral knee-dominant)
- **Definition**: Hip and knee flexion with upright torso, bilateral stance
- **Primary movers**: Quadriceps, glutes
- **Examples**: Back squat, front squat, goblet squat, leg press, wall sit
- **Function**: Sit-to-stand, ground-to-standing, athletic positions
- **Frequency**: 1-3x per week depending on goals

**2. Hinge Pattern** (Hip-dominant)
- **Definition**: Hip flexion with minimal knee bend, posterior chain emphasis
- **Primary movers**: Hamstrings, glutes, erectors
- **Examples**: Deadlift (conventional, sumo, trap bar), RDL, good mornings, hip thrusts
- **Function**: Picking things up, bending over, jumping, sprinting
- **Frequency**: 1-3x per week depending on goals

**3. Push (Horizontal)**
- **Definition**: Pushing away from body horizontally
- **Primary movers**: Pectorals, anterior deltoids, triceps
- **Examples**: Bench press, push-ups, dumbbell press (flat/incline), cable chest press
- **Function**: Pushing objects, getting up from ground
- **Frequency**: 1-3x per week

**4. Push (Vertical)**
- **Definition**: Pushing overhead
- **Primary movers**: Deltoids (all heads), triceps, upper traps
- **Examples**: Overhead press, push press, landmine press, dumbbell shoulder press
- **Function**: Lifting objects overhead, athletic movements
- **Frequency**: 1-2x per week (less than horizontal due to shoulder stress)

**5. Pull (Horizontal)**
- **Definition**: Pulling toward body horizontally
- **Primary movers**: Lats, rhomboids, rear delts, biceps
- **Examples**: Barbell rows, dumbbell rows, cable rows, inverted rows
- **Function**: Pulling objects, rowing, climbing
- **Frequency**: 1-3x per week (should match or exceed pushing)

**6. Pull (Vertical)**
- **Definition**: Pulling downward from overhead
- **Primary movers**: Lats, teres major, biceps
- **Examples**: Pull-ups, chin-ups, lat pulldowns
- **Function**: Climbing, hanging, pulling down objects
- **Frequency**: 1-3x per week

**7. Loaded Carry / Anti-Movement**
- **Definition**: Maintaining position under load or resisting unwanted movement
- **Primary movers**: Core (all), stabilizers
- **Examples**:
  - Loaded carry: Farmer's walks, suitcase carry, overhead carry
  - Anti-extension: Plank variations, dead bugs, ab wheel
  - Anti-rotation: Pallof press, bird dogs, single-arm farmer's walk
  - Anti-flexion: Back extensions, superman holds
- **Function**: Trunk stability, injury prevention, functional strength
- **Frequency**: 2-7x per week (can train core more frequently)

### Optional: Unilateral / Single-Leg
- **Definition**: Single-leg variations of squat/hinge patterns
- **Examples**: Lunges, split squats, step-ups, single-leg RDL
- **Benefits**: Balance, lateral stability, address asymmetries
- **Frequency**: 1-2x per week (or integrated into squat/hinge work)

## Balance Requirements

### Program-Level Balance (Full Program)

**Minimum Coverage**:
✅ At least ONE exercise from each of the 7 patterns
✅ Push:Pull ratio 1:1 or favoring pulling (1:1.5 or 1:2 for shoulder health)
✅ Horizontal and vertical pushing both represented
✅ Horizontal and vertical pulling both represented
✅ Squat and hinge both represented
✅ Core/anti-movement work included

**Ideal Coverage**:
- Squat: 2-3 variations (e.g., back squat, goblet squat, Bulgarian split squat)
- Hinge: 2-3 variations (e.g., deadlift, RDL, hip thrust)
- Horizontal push: 2-3 exercises
- Vertical push: 1-2 exercises
- Horizontal pull: 2-3 exercises (match or exceed horizontal push)
- Vertical pull: 1-2 exercises
- Core: 3-5 exercises (various anti-movement patterns)
- Unilateral: 1-2 exercises

### Session-Level Balance (Individual Workout)

**Principles**:
1. **Don't need ALL patterns every session** (that's what weekly balance is for)
2. **DO balance antagonist patterns within session** if both present
3. **Pull volume ≥ Push volume** to protect shoulders

**Upper Body Session Example**:
```
Horizontal push: Bench press 4x5
Horizontal pull: Barbell row 4x8 (matching or exceeding push volume)
Vertical push: Overhead press 3x8
Vertical pull: Pull-ups 3x6-8 (matching vertical push)
Core: Planks 3x30s

✅ Balanced: Push and pull matched, both planes covered
```

**Lower Body Session Example**:
```
Squat: Back squat 5x5 (knee-dominant)
Hinge: RDL 3x8 (hip-dominant)
Unilateral: Bulgarian split squats 2x10 each
Core: Dead bugs 3x10

✅ Balanced: Squat and hinge both present, unilateral included
```

**Mixed Session Example** (Full body):
```
Primary squat: Front squat 4x6
Primary hinge: Deadlift 3x5
Horizontal push: Bench 3x8
Horizontal pull: Dumbbell rows 3x10
Vertical pull: Pull-ups 3x6
Core: Pallof press 3x12

✅ Balanced: All major patterns, push:pull = 1:1.5 (favoring pull)
```

### Push:Pull Ratio for Shoulder Health

**Guidelines**:
- **Minimum**: 1:1 (equal push and pull)
- **Recommended**: 1:1.5 to 1:2 (favoring pull)
- **Rationale**: Pulling strengthens posterior shoulder and scapular stabilizers, counteracts modern posture (desk work, phone use), prevents impingement

**Calculation**:
```
Total push volume = (Horizontal push sets) + (Vertical push sets)
Total pull volume = (Horizontal pull sets) + (Vertical pull sets)
Ratio = Total push : Total pull

Example:
Push: Bench 4 sets + OHP 3 sets = 7 sets
Pull: Rows 5 sets + Pull-ups 3 sets = 8 sets
Ratio: 7:8 (approximately 1:1.15, acceptable)

Better:
Push: Bench 4 sets + OHP 3 sets = 7 sets
Pull: Rows 6 sets + Pull-ups 4 sets = 10 sets
Ratio: 7:10 (1:1.43, excellent for shoulder health)
```

## Validation Criteria

### Program-Level Validation
✅ All 7 fundamental patterns represented at least once per week
✅ Push:Pull ratio 1:1 minimum (preferably 1:1.5 or better)
✅ Horizontal AND vertical push both present
✅ Horizontal AND vertical pull both present
✅ Squat AND hinge both present (not just one)
✅ Core/anti-movement work included (2+ times per week)

### Session-Level Validation
✅ If push present, pull also present (antagonist balance)
✅ Pull volume ≥ Push volume within session (if both present)
✅ No excessive focus on one pattern (e.g., all push, no pull)

### Warning Signs
⚠️ Push:Pull ratio >1:1 (favoring push - shoulder injury risk)
⚠️ Only squat OR hinge (not both) - imbalanced lower body
⚠️ No core work for multiple weeks
⚠️ All vertical movements, no horizontal (or vice versa)
⚠️ No unilateral work ever (asymmetry risk)

### Automatic Failures
❌ Missing multiple fundamental patterns entirely (e.g., no hinging at all)
❌ Push:Pull ratio >2:1 (excessive push volume, high injury risk)
❌ Only one plane of movement (only horizontal, no vertical)
❌ No core work in program

## Examples

### Example 1: 4-Day Upper/Lower Split
**Program Structure**:
```
Monday - Lower A:
- Squat: Back squat 5x5 (squat pattern)
- Hinge: RDL 3x8 (hinge pattern)
- Unilateral: Lunges 2x10 each (unilateral)
- Core: Plank 3x30s (anti-extension)

Tuesday - Upper A:
- Horizontal push: Bench press 5x5
- Horizontal pull: Barbell row 5x8 (matching volume)
- Vertical pull: Pull-ups 3x6
- Core: Pallof press 3x12 (anti-rotation)

Thursday - Lower B:
- Hinge: Deadlift 4x5 (hinge pattern)
- Squat: Goblet squat 3x10 (squat pattern)
- Unilateral: Bulgarian split squat 2x10 (unilateral)
- Core: Bird dogs 3x10 (anti-rotation + anti-extension)

Saturday - Upper B:
- Vertical push: Overhead press 4x8
- Horizontal pull: Dumbbell rows 4x10 (exceeding push volume)
- Horizontal push: Incline bench 3x10
- Vertical pull: Lat pulldowns 3x12
- Core: Dead bugs 3x10 (anti-extension)
```

**Validation**:
- ✅ Squat: 2x/week (Mon, Thu)
- ✅ Hinge: 2x/week (Mon, Thu)
- ✅ Horizontal push: 2x/week (Tue, Sat)
- ✅ Vertical push: 1x/week (Sat)
- ✅ Horizontal pull: 2x/week (Tue, Sat)
- ✅ Vertical pull: 2x/week (Tue, Sat)
- ✅ Core: 4x/week (all sessions)
- ✅ Unilateral: 2x/week (Mon, Thu)
- ✅ Push:Pull = (5+5+4+3):(5+3+4+3) = 17:15 ≈ 1:0.88 → **Adjustment needed**, increase pull slightly

**Adjustment**:
Add 1 set to rowing movements → 17:17 = 1:1 (acceptable, or add more pull for 1:1.2)

### Example 2: 3-Day Full Body
**Program Structure**:
```
Monday:
- Squat: Front squat 4x6
- Horizontal pull: Barbell row 4x8
- Vertical push: Overhead press 3x8
- Core: Plank 3x30s

Wednesday:
- Hinge: Deadlift 4x5
- Horizontal push: Bench press 4x8
- Vertical pull: Pull-ups 3x6
- Core: Pallof press 3x12

Friday:
- Squat: Goblet squat 3x12
- Hinge: RDL 3x10
- Horizontal push: Dumbbell bench 3x10
- Horizontal pull: Dumbbell row 3x12
- Vertical pull: Lat pulldown 3x10
- Core: Dead bugs 3x10
```

**Validation**:
- ✅ Squat: 2 sessions (Mon, Fri)
- ✅ Hinge: 2 sessions (Wed, Fri)
- ✅ Horizontal push: 2 sessions (Wed, Fri)
- ✅ Vertical push: 1 session (Mon)
- ✅ Horizontal pull: 2 sessions (Mon, Fri)
- ✅ Vertical pull: 2 sessions (Wed, Fri)
- ✅ Core: 3 sessions (all days)
- ✅ Push:Pull = (3+4+3):(4+3+3+3) = 10:13 ≈ 1:1.3 (excellent!)

### Example 3: Imbalanced Program (NEEDS FIX)
**Program Structure** (problematic):
```
Monday:
- Bench press 5x5
- Incline bench 4x8
- Overhead press 3x10
- Tricep extensions 3x12

Wednesday:
- Squat 5x5
- Leg press 3x10
- Leg extensions 3x12
- Calf raises 3x15

Friday:
- Bench press 4x8
- Dumbbell flyes 3x12
- Lateral raises 3x12
- Tricep dips 3x10
```

**Issues Identified**:
❌ NO pulling movements at all (huge imbalance, shoulder injury risk)
❌ NO hinging movements (only squatting for lower body)
❌ NO vertical pulling
❌ Push:Pull ratio = infinite (all push, no pull)
❌ Minimal core work

**Fixed Version**:
```
Monday:
- Bench press 4x5
- Barbell row 5x8 (add horizontal pull)
- Overhead press 3x8
- Pull-ups 3x6 (add vertical pull)
- Core: Plank 3x30s

Wednesday:
- Squat 4x5
- Deadlift 3x5 (add hinge pattern)
- Leg curls 3x10 (posterior chain)
- Core: Bird dogs 3x10

Friday:
- Incline bench 3x8
- Dumbbell rows 4x10 (add horizontal pull, exceed push volume)
- Lat pulldowns 3x12 (add vertical pull)
- Face pulls 3x20 (posterior shoulder health)
- Core: Dead bugs 3x10
```

**Now Validated**:
- ✅ All patterns present
- ✅ Push:Pull = (4+3+3):(5+3+4+3) = 10:15 = 1:1.5 (excellent)
- ✅ Hinge added (deadlift)
- ✅ Core work all sessions

## Anti-Patterns

❌ **All push, no pull** - Recipe for shoulder impingement and poor posture
❌ **Only squatting, no hinging** - Neglects posterior chain, hamstring imbalance
❌ **Only hinging, no squatting** - Neglects quadriceps, knee health
❌ **No core work** - Spine injury risk, poor power transfer
❌ **Only horizontal movements** (no vertical) - Incomplete shoulder development
❌ **Only vertical movements** (no horizontal) - Incomplete development
❌ **No unilateral work** - Asymmetries can develop unchecked
❌ **Push:Pull > 1.5:1** (favoring push) - Shoulder injury risk increases significantly
